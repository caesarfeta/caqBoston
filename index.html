What is Capoeira?

Capoeira is a Brazilian martial art, hidden in dance. It came to Brazil when slaves were brought from Africa during the mid 1500s. After the abolition of slavery, capoeira survived in the poorer neighborhoods (despite persecution from police) until becoming legalized in the 1930's. Today, capoeira is taught around the world, with each group incorporating its own blend of movements, music, and philosophy.

CAQ Boston

We train and play in a supportive environment that emphasizes a thoughtful, creative, and technically sophisticated approach to capoeira. Most classes begin with music training, then movement training, and finally a roda (pronounced: "HO-duh"), where we all play each other applying what we practice.

Our Lineage

Mestre Ombrinho

The head instructor at the New York Capoeira Center is Mestre Ombrinho. With over 30 years practicing capoeira and over 24 years experience teaching, he is the first North American Capoeira Master. He is renowned for making the art of Capoeira accessible to people of all ages and abilities. He began studying Capoeira in 1981 with Mestre Acordeon and Mestre Jelon, two internationally-recognized masters who brought capoeira to the United States. He made his first trip to Brazil in 1985 where he trained with many legendary masters, young and old. Having trained many capoeira styles including Regional, which emphasizes fighting aspects, and Angola, which emphasizes a more thoughtful game, Mestre Ombrinho was drawn to the latter and formed Capoeira Angola Quintal NY.

Contra-Mestre Graveto

Contra-Mestre Graveto has studied and trained under Mestre Ombrinho, Mestre Nô, Contra-Mestre Pererê for over 15 years and has dedicated himself to the continuation of Capoeira Angola and in enriching the lives of those that it touches. In addition to leading a group in Ithaca, he provides intensive guidance to students around the country including CAQ Boston.

Music

Music is just as important as movement in our capoeira practice. The songs, sung in Portuguese, transmit capoeira's history and philosophy. They are played with traditional instruments and employ different rhythms, which are carefully used to establish and react to the tone of the game. The music helps create the movement and the movement helps create the music. Through our music practice we learn how to harmonize and improvise with the people around us inside and outside of the roda.

Culture

Capoeira is a centuries old tradition, which began in Africa, was brought to Brazil, and is now spreading all over the world. It has survived repression from colonial slave-owners and authoritarian governments. CAQ Boston is a part of capoeira's deep tradition and continuing evolution. Whether a beginner or a mestre, we all help create great capoeira by sharing our sounds and movements.
